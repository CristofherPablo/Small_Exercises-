The weight and size of wolves vary greatly throughout the world, tending to increase proportionally with latitude, as predicted by the theory of Christian Bergmann. Generally, the height, measured from the shoulders, ranges from 60 to 95 centimeters.

The weight varies geographically. On average, European wolves weigh 38.5 kg; North American wolves weigh 36 kg; Indian and Arabian wolves weigh 25 kg. Although rare, wolves weighing more than 77 kg have been found in Alaska, Canada, and the former Soviet Union.

Wolves are social animals that live in packs, which are usually composed of a mated pair and their offspring. They are known for their distinctive howling, which is used to communicate with other members of the pack over long distances.

Wolves are carnivorous and primarily hunt large prey such as deer, elk, and moose, although they will also eat smaller mammals such as rodents and rabbits.
